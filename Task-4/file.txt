The Musical Sucker: A Lollipop That Plays Music in Your Skull
At the 2026 Consumer Electronics Show, the tech world was introduced to the "Lollipop Star," a gadget that finally answers the question: "Can I listen to my favorite tracks using only my teeth?" This $9 treat uses bone conduction technology to send sound vibrations through your jawbone directly to your inner ear.This means that while you enjoy a sugar fix, the music plays "inside your head," completely silent to anyone standing next to you. It’s essentially a personal concert where the ticket price is a potential cavity.


The device currently offers a limited "menu" of artists, including Akon and Ice Spice, with each artist paired with a specific candy flavor. To make it work, you simply pair the base with your phone and start snacking. Tech reviewers who tested it noted the surreal experience of "tasting" a beat, though some raised concerns about the social etiquette of staring intensely into space while aggressively sucking on a musical lollipop in public.
